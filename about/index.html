About

I grew up in Chengdu, Sichuan Province, China. I spent my college life at a Sino-French Institute of Sun Yat-sen University and then continued pursuing an academic career in France. My doctoral studies were completed at the CEA-Saclay (Commissariat à l’Energie Atomique et aux Energies Alternatives) center in Paris under the direction of professor Charlotte BECQUART of Lille University and professor Frédéric SOISSON at the SRMP of CEA-Saclay. My research focuses on the atomistic modeling of thermodynamic properties, diffusion and ordering in Fe-Ni alloys.

I am particularly drawn to researches about multi-scale modeling of materials and radiation-induced damage simulations. I am skilled in scientific coding, modeling, material simulations and data analysis. On this site, some of my researches and events that I find interesting or worthy will be presented.
